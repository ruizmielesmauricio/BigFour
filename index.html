Big Four Thrash Dashboard

Interactive analysis of Metallica, Megadeth, Slayer and Anthrax

Project Overview

This dashboard explores the musical and audience dynamics of the “Big Four” of thrash metal — Metallica, Megadeth, Slayer, and Anthrax — by combining data from multiple sources including Last.fm, MusicBrainz, and GetSongBPM. It provides an interactive environment to analyze popularity, discography trends, and musical characteristics such as tempo (BPM) across bands and albums.

The application highlights key metrics such as total streams, listener counts, and average tempo, allowing users to compare artists and albums in a meaningful way. It identifies top-performing tracks and albums, visualizes differences in musical speed, and enables direct album-to-album comparisons.

Data was collected and merged from multiple APIs and datasets, then cleaned and enriched to ensure consistency across artists, albums, and tracks. Feature engineering was applied to calculate metrics such as average BPM per album and aggregated playcounts.

The analysis reveals clear differences in popularity and musical style between the bands. Metallica consistently dominates in total streams and audience reach, while bands like Slayer stand out for higher average tempo.

The project was built using Python, Pandas, Plotly, and Streamlit. The final application is deployed on Streamlit Cloud and integrated into this portfolio page for easy access.
